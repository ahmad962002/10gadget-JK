    Bed Leveling
    For 3D printing one hurdle to overcome is to get the first layer of melted filament goo to stick to the print bed. To do that you need to set the print nozzle to an appropriate height above each portion of the print bed using the four level adjusting screws on the print bed corners. Turn on the printer and use the control knob on the printer to sent the print head to the "Home" position with the nozzle in the lower left corner of the print bed just above the print bed. Then turn off the printer and adjust the screws to lower the bed at each corner enough to slip a sheet of typing paper under the nozzle with slight resistance. You can manually depress the print bed and push it and the nozzle around to test the nozzle height at each corner of the bed. Although the instructions say that the paper should slide with slight resistance under the nozzle, I found that for printing with PLA filament, I had to lower the bed by an extra 1/4 turn on each adjusting screw in order to get the goo to stick well on the bed. Test print a small component after leveling the bed and adjust the bed height if the goo is not sticking properly.

    Bed Surface
    The other thing you need to get the goo to stick well is a suitable bed surface. People use painter's tape, or glass with hairspray or glue stick on the surface of the print bed to aid in goo sticking Although the Monoprice printer in the past apparently came with some painter's tape on the bed, my printer arrived covered with black plastic looking material on the bed. I think is "BuildTak", or something similar. Whatever it is, it works exceptionally well. Once I got the bed height properly adjusted, the printer has had no problems getting any print to stick.
