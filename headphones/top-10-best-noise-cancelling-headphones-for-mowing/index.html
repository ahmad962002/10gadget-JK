    These are not meant to be noise canceling headphones, these are ear protection muffs that have an added Bluetooth feature for convenience. Noise canceling earphones work by playing the sound waves captured by the mic in reverse so the sound gets canceled.

    These earmuffs use sound isolation to reduce noise, this is pretty much just using materials that cover the ear to block sound naturally, plugging your ears with your fingers would be considered noise isolation. Noise isolation earmuffs or ear plugs have a noise blocking rating in "dB", this is just a measurement of noise. You can find charts online that describe what sounds your dB rating could block, these have 26dB.

    Passive sound is just a feature that lets outside sounds captured by the mic to be played through the speakers. You still get the noise reduction from the ear cups physically blocking your ears but get normal levels of ambient sound playing through the speakers as if the outside noise was low. Taking calls works the same as the passive feature but you are just listening to a call instead of surrounding sounds.

    The sound quality on these for music is good enough but this is just and added feature, if you are working somewhere where ear protection is required then you probably wont be listening to music anyways. The build of the earmuffs is pretty decent, the thing i dont like is the plastic pieces that connect the cups to the headband, to me they look like the weak point of the earmuffs.

    VERY IMPORTANT-One thing that you should check after getting these is that the pads make a good seal. I had to replace the first pair I received because the left pad had an air leak.
