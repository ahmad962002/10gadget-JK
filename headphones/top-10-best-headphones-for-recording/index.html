    I am a classic music lover, and this product fits my needs perfectly. I was tired of putting up with shoddy designs that fall apart. Often it is the foam isolation that fails. In my lab I located a worn-out pair of Bose earphones, and I was determined to effect some kind of repair. I invented a new design that yields an earphone that is at least the equal of the best on the market, including Bose, Sennheiser, and even ATH.

    I will tell you how to do it.

    I begin by pulling apart the sticky elements that hold the earphone together, until I am left with a foam ring and a few pieces of cloth. The process continues --

    1. Stick the foam ring and pieces of cloth back onto the earphone cup.

    2. Mount a single layer of Beige Masking Tape over the cup, pulling on the tape edges to create a flat surface. An overlap of perhaps 3/16 inch between pieces of tape can be used.

    3. Fold the edges of the tape around the edge of the cup, to hold it in place.

    4. Apply a thin ring of Gorilla Expanding Glue to the surface of the tape and use your forefinger to distribute it uniformly over the entire surface in the thinnest possible layer. You want to minimize the weight of the resulting membrane.

    5. Set aside to dry - about two hours. I did this five times to perfect my technique.

    This process has unexpected consequences. The masking tape acts as a membrane interface between the cup and the ear. Before coating, the tape is floppy with poor frequency response. But as the glue dries, it permeates the tape, creating a semi-transparent membrane with perfect compliance to the sound wave. This may be observed in process.

    As the glue dries, the frequency response suddenly increases to equal that of the best of good headphones, and the new membrane will respond to every nuance in the available sound. Expanding Gorilla Glue is a MAGIC component in this design. The combination of Gorilla Glue and Masking Tape yields a new membrane that is unsurpassed in acoustical properties.

    An even greater surprise comes when we see there is no aperture in the new membrane. The cup is completely covered with a continuous membrane that delivers complete
    compliance with the sound wave. Then the great rolling bass and bell-like treble of the Steinway Model D concert grand piano is fully revealed.
