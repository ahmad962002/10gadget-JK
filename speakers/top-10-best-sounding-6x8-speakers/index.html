A brief note about small full range speakers... (I did 20 years in commercial sound system installation in auditoriums/theaters etc.)... Don't always assume that "more is better" in the sense that "triaxial" or "quad axial" car speakers are going to sound better than coaxials like these (2-way with woofer & tweeter). Typically those tri/quad axial speakers (especially those with metal foil tweeter domes) are HARSH, metallic and unnaturally loud in the highs (in my opinion giving the auditory equivalent of pouring rubbing alcohol on an open wound) and typically the high frequencies are unnaturally boosted way above the mid-range frequencies and it's not possible to tone them down with your treble knob or graphic EQ. A tri or quad axial setup is fine in a large PA speaker with an 18" & 10" speakers, a horn driver and some piezo tweeters for the crispies, but putting 2 or 3 different tweeters above a small car speaker is just a sales gimmick and will lead to more phase cancellation in the sound. A good set of 2-way/coaxials (woofer and tweeter) will typically give you a smoother, flatter-response sound that you won't have to have a 15 band EQ to tone down. OH AND ANOTHER THING...because these do have an impressive POWER HANDLING spec (60W/180W), this translates to them being a little less "efficient" (more power handling generally means the speakers have a heavier, more powerful magnet for the voice-coil, and the speaker cone is thicker and heavier, so the same amount of electrical energy won't throw the speaker back and forth as aggressively as a lighter magnet/coil/speaker cone would. Therefor these speakers may not seem quite as loud as your original speakers (if they were lighter more "efficient" ones), however you will likely have BETTER AND SMOOTHER sound and better speaker durability due to that small step down in efficiency. In my car these speakers were about 20% less loud than the original cheap factory speakers, but the sound is WAY WAY WAY better quality and the system will still go louder than I'd ever use it anyway. VERY GOOD AFFORDABLE SPEAKERS for the right application and right listener. RECOMMENDED.

FAQS

What are good 6x9 bass speakers?

The JBL GTO609C is a great 6x9 bass speaker. It has a frequency response of 30-21,000 Hz and can handle up to 400 watts of power.

What is the best brand of 6x9 speakers?

There isn't any definitive answer as the best brand of 6x9 speakers, as different people might have different opinions on this topic. Some popular brands of 6x9 speakers include JBL, Pioneer, and Polk Audio. It really depends on what kind of sound you are looking for and what your budget is.

Are factory speakers better than aftermarket?

Factory speakers are typically better than aftermarket in terms of quality. This is because the factory speaker has been specifically designed for the car it is going in, whereas an aftermarket speaker may not be.

Do 3-way speakers sound better than 2 way?

It depends on the speakers. Usually, 3-way speakers sound better because they offer more clarity and separation of the frequencies.
