    I have been wearing this watch for a little more than a week now, so this initial review will be based on that, I will try to remember to update this after a month and conclude what I really think of this watch.

    So far my initial impression of the watch is good. When I first handled it, I was impressed by the heft and finish of the case. It is very solid, no strange, gaping seams or jagged edges that taint it's perfect roundness. I noticed that the second hand was already running, this is because you can't "hack" the watch when you are adjusting the minute hand. The hands are easily read because of the contrast of it's white against the black face. The inner bezel surrounding the watch face is polished and has a nice reflection which adds to the shape. If it weren't for that bezel, I think it would look very dull.

    Many people complain about the size of the watch, but in it's defense the measurements are stated upfront. There should not have been any other expectations. But it does look smaller than stated. This is probably due to the face/outter bezel ratio. The outter bezel is about 3.5mm which leaves the face and inner bezel to have a diameter of about 30mm.

    The face is very clear and easy to read thanks to the white on black combination as well as the hardlex crystal. The product photo doesn't do the watch much justice because in person, the black dial is a very rich black. The lume isn't that impressive. In a regular situation, the lume will probably not be charged enough to really notice in a dim/dark room. But for testing purposes, I charged it up with a flashlight. The lume will probably last about 10 minutes till it diminishes.

    Setting the watch was fairly simple. The crown is a little difficult to handle because of its position in the case. But even when it has been lifted, the increments from the first position to the second position is very small. The firmness of the crown makes it a little difficult to determine which position you are in. Setting the date looked like a troubling task to some of the other reviewers, but it wasn't hard to figure out. When in the 2nd crown position, you rotate the crown clockwise(upward) to change the day and counter-clockwise(downward) to change the date. I hear from other forums to avoid doing this between 9PM and 3AM since the day/date starts to turn between those hours and adjusting it might cause some issues.

    The strap that was supplied didn't impress me much at first. Sometimes it would look really deep black, and sometimes it just looks ragged depending on the light. The length is substantial, I am sure it can accommodate most people wearing this watch. I have thin wrist and it looks great on me. But since it is longer, the end of the strap is left to protrude a little more than I prefer. It also is a little coarse. It isn't uncomfortable, but looks like it would be. Which, in some cases, could be worse. I was willing to tolerate this but thought, if this is a watch I want to wear everyday, I will probably change it out to something nicer. I ordered a black leather band to compliment the face of the watch.

    As far as accuracy, the first couple days fluctuated from +1 to +5. Which isn't bad at all. But it wasn't consistent. So I looked into it a little bit. During regular wear, sitting at my computer, driving, etc., it would maybe gain 1 second or less by the end of the day. I check periodically against my phone's clock. but one night, I decided to wind it up aggressively, and realized by doing so, it gained about 6 seconds over 2-3 hours. So my conclusion to that was not to aggressively wind it and just let it wind through regular wear. The first few nights I let it rest on it's back and found that by the next morning, it gained 5 seconds. I reset it back for testing purposes and tried leaving it in a different position the following night. And to my surprise, it didn't gain any noticeable time at all. If there was any time drift at all it might have been a -.25second change. So aside from the initial +1 to +5 gain it naturally has, I was able to find a way to keep it even more accurate.

    TIP: Try leaving it in different positions overnight if you don't plan to sleep with it on. I read some people have luck resting it faced down. But for me it was to have it set perpendicular with the 6 positioned down.

    UPDATE: It has been about a month since I have been wearing this watch day-to-day. I have to really express how impressed I am of the quality. The case is very durable and the crystal is still spotless. Not to say that I have been crawling through mud or rock climbing with it on or anything. But from my everyday activities, normal office type work, and just running errands throughout the day, the watch has probably been bumped and grazed a few times and has not left any marks.

    As for the time drifting, my initial observations are still true. In my case, leaving the watch face up will gain a few seconds overnight. Leaving it upright will lose less than a second. And just throughout the day, it might gain/lose a second. This is considered to be VERY accurate. My results may differ from others.

    I got rid of the strap it came with in favor of a light weight resin strap instead. The original canvas strap was not comfortable and held water. The resin strap made this watch more tolerable and easier to dry after washing my hands. I inadvertently tested the water resistance from a couple quick splashes, held up nicely.

    The only thing that still bugs me about the watch, and this may be true to all automatics but I will state it anyway, is that the winding noise is very present and makes the watch seem imperfect. But that does not affect my admiration for the watch. I enjoy wearing this watch everyday.

    Update: It's been about two years since I've owned this and the accuracy is pretty far off. I haven't worn it much lately but still have it in my rotation of watches. It gains about 2 minutes a day now. Might be due for some maintenance.
