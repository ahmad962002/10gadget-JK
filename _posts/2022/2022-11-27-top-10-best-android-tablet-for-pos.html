One thing I realized right away was that I had to be logged into my Google account at all times and that gave me an uneasiness of feeling like a data-mine. I also tried playing games on it but it just didn't work out. The feeling of gaming on it is super awkward and sometimes the games would lag.

    Alright now let's move on to the tablet/keyboard side of it. The keyboard is a lot flimsier than I expected. The tablet itself is very bulky compared to mainstream tablets like iPads. The back cover is completely magnetic and can come off entirely which happened a few times when I was trying to set it upright. One important thing this device made me realize was that I'm just not into tablets.

    I thought this product would help substitute my laptops but instead I learned a very important lesson from all this. Be happy with what you've got. Some things are just not worth it. If you've used Chromebooks or tablets with detachable keyboards before, then maybe this device won't be a problem for you. But if you've never used either one, then let this be a cautionary tale.
