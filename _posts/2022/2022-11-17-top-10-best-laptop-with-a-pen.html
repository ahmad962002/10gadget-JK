    UPDATE: I found a fix to the audio issue. I'm not 100% on the order of operations, since I changed a lot of things, but if you are experiencing issues, try this:

    1. Go to device settings > Sounds, video, and game controllers
    2. Select the RealTek audio device and right-click to update the driver
    3. Choose browse my computer for drivers
    4. Choose let me pick from a list
    5. Install the High Definition Audio Device drivers (restart if asked)
    6. Go to the registry editor and type "Computer\HKEY_LOCAL_MACHINE\SYSTEM" into the box at the top. Hit enter.
    7.Hit ctrl + f to bring up search and type "PowerSettings" Hit enter.
    8. You should end up in a subfolder of 0001 or 0002. Click on the parent folder (the 0001 or 0002 folder) and look at the DriverDesc field. Find the one that says "AMD High Definition Audio"
    9. Now go to the PowerSettings subfolder for that one.
    10. Modify the "ConservationIdleTime" file to be all zeros: (00 00 00 00) and click OK.
    11. Audio issue should be fixed now, but restarting the laptop is a good idea.
    12. I then went online and downloaded the latest AMD drivers. Just google "amd ryzen 5 4500u drivers" and it should be the first results. Click Drivers and Support at the top of the page and download the software.
    13. Restart the laptop after installing the AMD updated software.

    Hope this helps someone.
