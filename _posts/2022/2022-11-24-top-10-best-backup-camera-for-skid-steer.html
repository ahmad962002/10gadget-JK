    3 main steps:

    1) decide where you want the monitor so you know where to run the wire upfront.

    2) remove a rear taillamp ( I removed the drivers side) to access the backup light wiring., identify the reverse power wire and the ground.     3) run the wire from the front to the rear. I ran mine along the headliner. Some run the wire along the floor, but I think foot traffic could damage the wire.... Whereas along the headliner has no risk of that failure mode.

I ran the wire out to the license plate following the license plate lighting circuit. I then cut a very small slot alongside the license plate light. In this way, the wiring is sealed, no water entry risk.

FAQS

Which is better wired or wireless backup camera?

Wireless backup cameras transmit video signals through the air, while wired backup cameras use a physical connection to send video signals. Many people feel that wireless backup cameras are better because they are easier to set up and don't require any extra cords or cables.

What is best wireless backup camera?

If you are looking for the best wireless backup camera then the Circle 2 is the perfect option. It is very easy to set up and provides a great view of what is behind your vehicle.

What is the best viewing angle for a backup camera?

The best viewing angle for a backup camera is when the camera is placed at a 45 degree angle to the ground.

Is rear view monitor the same as a backup camera?

Rear view monitors and backup cameras are not the same. A rear view monitor is a device that displays an image of what is behind your vehicle when reversing. A backup camera, on the other hand, is a camera that is installed in or near the rear license plate area of a vehicle to provide an image of what is behind the vehicle when it is in reverse gear.
