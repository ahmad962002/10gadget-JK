    FULL SPECTRUM - Full Spectrum mode adds a fuzziness or haze to the image. The IR shots are much cleaner than the Full Spectrum. I have tens of thousands of images from investigations with hundreds of apparitions in infrared. I've yet to capture anything in ultra-violet. Full Spectrum is made by merging 3 separate images - one from the near-IR range, one from the visible range, and one from the near-ultraviolet range. That's why the image looks fuzzier, these are different focal lengths shot in one focal plane so of course it would be a little fuzzy. I'm not convinced yet that there is any value to Full Spectrum. IR is a snap, push a button on the back of the camera. When I’m out on a hunt being able to tap one button that turns on the infrared light and switches to black & white mode is sweet. Full Spectrum, however, requires doing that, plus going into the menu settings under ‘Color’, and changing the setting to ‘Vivid’. So for me, I use just the single, built-in lens, and switch between visible light and IR by using the simple push button. This also makes the internal IR light usable again as it is no longer blocked by the wide angle lens. Additional information about full spectrum from Wikipedia says: It is important to note that while the converted camera sensor is capable of recording in both the ultraviolet and infrared region, when mixed light hits the sensor it will be the longer infrared waves that will predominate in the recording. Little or no shortwave ultraviolet light may be recorded unless selective filtering is applied to cut some or all of the infrared light. The longwave infrared light may also wash out a considerable amount of the visible light in the blue and green areas in a full spectrum photograph. Similarly if infrared light is entirely blocked, the visible light can overwhelm the recording of the ultraviolet light. So there is no truly full-spectrum photograph that can be made [with this camera. Good full spectrum cameras exist and the standard method is to shoot the still-subject well-focused three times, with three different filters and merging the 3 images into one. Video requires a complicated method of realtime splitting of the image 3 ways, applying the appropriate filter, then recombining before saving. $$$. This camera has no manual focus, it is set for infinite focus.]
