    I contacted SUNLU requesting guidance and they told me the print head temp and build plate temp () need to be higher than normal (210-230C and 60-80C respectively) for PLA SILK vs. standard PLA.     I made the changes. First print had some very minor bed adhesion but the print was able to recover and finished the print very nicely. Print number 2. No Bueno!.     Lessons learned:
    1. Be sure to verify recommended print temperatures on the box. They're not printed on the spool's label like everyone else. My fault. Felt really stupid when I found them on the second box.
    2. Be sure everything is a trammed and level as you can possible get them.
    3. Be sure your built plate is as clean as you can possibly get it
    4. Use higher temperatures than normal PLA. My temperatures ended up being:
    a. Printhead: 215-220C (anything higher made the sheen of the SIlk appear dull).
    b. Build plate: 65-70C, (70 was required before I gave it a thorough cleaning and used PVA glue).
    5. Layer height: .12mm (one of the magic numbers of my machine. Look up magic numbers if you're not familiar with them. Make printing appear nicer.).

FAQS

Is there a 3D printer that prints multiple colors?

There are multiple 3D printers that can print in multiple colors, including the DeltaWASP 20 40 3D printer, the FlashForge Creator Pro, and the Lulzbot Mini.

Are dual extruders worth it?

Dual extruders can be useful for a number of reasons, such as printing with two different colors or two different materials. However, they can also be more complicated to use and may not be worth the extra hassle for some users.

How many extruders can a 3D printer have?

A 3D printer typically has one extruder, but there are some printers that have two extruders.

Is it better to 3D print multiple parts at once?

It depends on the part's size and geometry. Printing multiple parts at once can save time and filament, but it can also lead to better quality prints.
