    At this juncture I want to refute the other two "faults", which other reviewers have ranted about. If the mount wobbles about the large T-knob screw clamp ( the T fastener closest to the auger ), then you simply haven't torqued it enough. And the 1/4-20 UNC threaded stud for the camera poses no serious problem, unless you lack the mechanical sense to :
    1) First back the wingnut well away from the camera.
    2) Then gently rotate the entire camera all the way down the threaded stud until it just "bottoms out". The wingnut should not be making contact.
    3) Next, back the camera out a partial-full turn so that it's pointing in the generally desired direction. Now you have the maximum thread engagement.
    4) Then back out the wingnut for whatever tightness against the camera you require; either temporarily or finally. And there's absolutely no need to add a jamnut to the assembly, as one mechanically challenged reviewer has remarked. After all, the wingnut already functions as a jamnut.

    It's impossible to damage either the camera or this nifty little mount if you follow the above "common" sense instructions.
