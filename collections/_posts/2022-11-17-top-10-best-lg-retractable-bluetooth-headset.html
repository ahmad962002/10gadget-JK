    BLUETOOTH CONNECTIVITY: 0/5

    The bread and butter of any bluetooth headset. My hope was that the bugs that were present in the 730 would have been ironed out in the 800. Sad to say I've been fairly disappointed. All the connectivity issues that I could have imagined and more are still present in the 800. I can go from sitting with my phone in my pocket and seemingly getting a stable connection, to standing up and walking around and the connection will grow unstable for several seconds and the sound will stutter. It's like they tried to do something clever and get the device to auto range it's Bluetooth signal to save battery power, except that when it has to adjust its signal power it's not a smooth transition and stuff just ends up garbled for several seconds. What irks me more though is that, albeit a rare occurrence, there have been a handful of times where the headset just stops outputting sound altogether and/or loses connection. Even if you're not moving and your phone is 2 ft. away from your head. The most recent instance of this is what compelled me to make this review. It's very annoying to compare the Bluetooth signal of the most recent model and have to say that it's outright inferior to that of the first model. I'd sooner opt for less battery life.

    SOUND: 5/5

    Probably the only area that surpasses the 700 model. The sound is great for this type of headset. It's clear and doesn't sound like it's being compressed. You can definitely tell that the 700 has a flatter sound by comparison. While I can't vouch for the sound clarity in comparison to the 730, it's definitely this items one saving grace.

    ANC: 1/5

    An interesting choice of addition to the headset, but it's nowhere near strong enough to be of any real use in noisy environments. Based on my usage of it, I'd say it'd be more accurate to judge it based on its ability to make quiet environments even quieter, which can be nice... But not an extra 70 bucks worth of nice. All in all this is more of an extra feature, and it's really not all that useful. It's worth noting as a side note though that the switch for ANC is separate from the rest of the button activated options that this device typically has. This means you can even turn ANC on(and keep it on) even when the device itself is turned off. Be careful, since this feature does drain battery life, if you accidentally forget to shut off ANC you'll probably end up regretting it if you still want to use the device later.

    PHONE CALLS: 5/5

    I don't typically make use of this device for calls, but I will say that the sound still comes out clear and the mic is good enough to not muffle everything you say.

    BATTERY LIFE: 3/5

    I turn it on at 5:30 in the morning, use it all throughout the day, and it's typically drained by 3:30. 10 hours of use when solely listening to music. Turn on ANC and have it on all day and you can cut 2 hours out of your battery life, so you're usually better off leaving ANC off. I don't have an estimate for making phone calls other than the battery seems to drain much faster when you're using it, so use it sparingly if you don't have access to a charger. The battery life is on par with what my 700 model was when it was still new. However, based on my observations of my family members 730 model, they can go about 11-12 hours based on music alone.

    DESIGN CHANGES: 4/5

    These took a bit of getting used to, but all in all I'd say it's a positive change. The LED's now off to the inside and doesn't stand out as much when you're wearing it. I've grown to like the new placement of the Vol. and the Fwd/Prev. buttons, as well as the sleeker look of the phone and play/pause buttons. I also like that the two small pieces holding the wire's to the earbuds are now thicker since the two small plastic pieces to hold the earbud wires eventually broke off my old 700 model. I will say I don't care for the lighter weight of the device. It feels kind of flimsy now and I'm wary of the possibility of it falling off and hitting the corner of a hard surface in the wrong way. Worthwhile changes were made overall I'd say, but part of me would actually prefer the LED's to be dimmer and less noticeable still.

    PRICE POINT: 0/5

    I'd like to say the price is a bad joke. At most I could see the overall usefulness of this being maybe $5-10 more than the 730, not twice the price and then some. Not for a device that I feel falls short of expectations along with needless additions. All in all, I'm not sure what the development team was thinking.

As in iron out the darn bugs and THEN expand on adding extra features like ANC. For this kind of price tag, what I was expecting was that at least the 3 core essentials of this product would've blown the 700 out of the water and at least put the 730 out of its misery. To specify, I was hoping the sound would've been better than even the 730, Bluetooth capability would've been as good if not better than the 700, and then battery life would've been a solid 12 hours minimum.
