    There are some very nice features to these buds, and I do intend to keep them. (Another reviewer was about to return them.) But there are some things that just aren't as good as I had hoped. I'll try to be as detailed as possible.

    First, the out-of-box experience is kinda nice, but not fabuous. The smallish box presents the much smaller charger box that the buds live in, and a tiny box that contains a charger cable. Wait -- are there really no instructions? And I thought there were interchangable ear thingies to accommodate ears of different sizes. Ok, so the very brief instruction book clings to the top of the box because it's too big not to fall out on its own. And the ear thingies are hiding in the box where the charging cable is. I seem to be ok with the default ear thingies that are already installed on the buds. (Note, you have to use your own charger with the charging cable. You probably already have too many of those.)

    You'll need to download and install the Galaxy Wearable app (on Android, a different one is available for iOS). The app helps you connect to the buds, pretty easy, and is reasonably fully functional. There are numerous options you can play with.

    The fit -- quite comfortable actually. Looking at the peculiar shape, I wondered whether these would stay in my ears, and whether they would be uncomfortable. Yes, they stay put, even when walking around, and no, not uncomfortable. This was a pleasant surprise; Samsung really got this part right. I feel as though I could wear these at the office all day and not get ear fatigue. Much, much better than a clumsy pair of headphones slipping off my head when I'm walking around. When wearing the buds, you can hear outside sounds/conversations very well, and perhaps that's the whole idea of these buds. I like this.

    There's an equalizer section on the app's main screen. The equalizer options are sort of ok, but not wonderful. I don't know why there's no option for a 10-band graphic equalizer. I just settled on the default after not liking the other choices any better.

    You can turn on and off the ANC (active noise cancellation). I have a pair of conventional headphones (not Bose) with ANC that works really well. You feel like you're in a cocoon. These buds have ANC that you can barely tell is working. It sounds a little different, maybe even a little bit of an improvement, but it doesn't seem like it's getting rid of any particular noise. So the ANC is sort of a bust.

    Now let's talk about sound quality. As I say, I really wanted to love these little guys. The sound quality is good but not great. Maybe I'm expecting too much. My pair of $250 conventional headphones sound spectacular, and I can listen to music for hours with them. These buds aren't that impressive. The sound quality is ok -- just ok. It's not "bad" in any way at all, but it's not great. I listened to an entire CD that I know well and like. The experience was pleasant, but at the end I really wanted to listen to it on the stereo instead. As I say, maybe I'm expecting too much. The sound quality is probably good enough to listen to at work all day. I had the volume up a little too high, and when my test CD was finished and I took the buds out of my ears, I felt that my ears were ringing a little bit. Next time I'll set the volume lower.

    As an interesting aside, I'll compare the sound quality to another wearable that I just got, the Bose Frames (audio sunglasses, a weirdly interesting product). The sound from the Bose Frames is "smooth" in a very wonderful way -- much better than the buds. However, the Bose Frames have absolutely no lower end -- no bass sounds at all. The sounds they do have are great. By contrast, these buds are full range, with pretty good bass. But the sound just isn't smooth.

    I didn't try to take a phone call., so I don't know how seamless this works. Considering it's Samsung, and I have a Galaxy S9, I bet this works well enough. I'm not very fussy about sound quality on calls -- I just need to be able to understand what's being said. So "good enough" for me is probably not hard to achieve.

    There's a "button" on the buds that you press to skip a track, answer a call, etc. It's not a real button, it's just a touch-sensitive area on the outside of the bud. I haven't been able to get this touch point to work reliably. I don't think there's necessarily anything wrong with the bud, probably just my fat fingers.

    Bottom line? I think I'll have a use for these, most likely when I want to listen to my own music without disturbing others, and without the very wonderful but cumbersome conventional earphones. When I'm walking out and about, the smooth sound from the Bose Frames is better. So it's a toss-up with the Galaxy Buds Live. They're good enough, but unfortunately not better than that.

    I am in love with the earbuds as far as color, shape, fit, sound, size, battery life, charging style, the app and several other things. HOWEVER, I believe the reviews/specs on Amazon showed multipoint capabilities and this is not true when using devices that are not Samsung. I really really do not want to return them but I think I have to because being able to seamlessly switch between devices is a must for me. I am so hurt. I don't understand why Samsung can't do multipoint with their earbuds when the most generic earbuds and earpieces have this capability.

    This is my second pair, my first I purchased directly from Samsung and I noticed some slight differences. The biggest is the battery.
