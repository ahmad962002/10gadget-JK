    There are tons of reviews, so I'm going to get straight to the point. There were a couple things that were required, and a couple things I wanted in a new monitor. Some context, I work from home and I'm getting my Masters degree online, so I often spend 50-60 hours a week at my computer. I wanted to get a monitor I would be happy with for work and play for several years. I like the ultrawide aspect ratio a lot, so that's what I was looking for primarily. I've now had the monitor for about 4 months.

    Required:
    - 34+ inches ultrawide
    - 75hz+ refresh rate
    - 1440p+ resolution
    - VESA mounting
    - Freesync/G-Sync
    - Good build quality/long-lasting
    - Curved (upgraded from a flat 34in monitor)
    Wanted:
    - 144hz+
    - Vibrant colors, good contrast
    - HDR
    - Thunderbolt docking

    First off, when I bought this monitor it was on sale for $650. It was a bit more than I wanted to pay, even though that is cheaper than regular price, but I decided to shell out the money because it hit all of my needs and most of my wants (I actually didn't notice that it didn't have HDR until after I bought it but that wasn't a deal breaker). I'll go through them and score them individually using a number of stars.

    -34 inch ultrawide (5*) - exactly what I wanted, nothing else to comment.
    -Refresh Rate (4*) - 100hz is the best I've ever had and I'm happy with it. More might be better, but I currently don't have a computer that can even handle 100hz all the time when gaming so it doesn't really make much of a difference, however I recognize it might be lacking for some people.
    -Resolution (5*) - I just upgraded from a 1080p, and I don't have a fantastic GPU, so again, I'm happy with it for just about everything.
    -VESA mount (2*) - Not my favorite. Instead of just putting the mount right on the back like most monitors, this one has an adapter that attaches to the existing adjustable arm. I have this mounted to my own monitor arm. What that means is that there are now 2 pivot points for vertical adjustment on this monitor, one on the monitor itself, and one on the arm. It is functional, but I would much rather just mount right to the back of the monitor instead.
    -Freesync (3*) - Freesync doesn't work through the thunderbolt connection, and this was not mentioned anywhere. I could have done my own research to discover that, so I recognize this is partially on me, but it should be made clear and that bugs me that it was not mentioned anywhere.
    -Build Quality (4*) - I haven't had any issues with this, it seems to be working fine and not showing any signs of wearing out, but there is a lot of plastic, and I'm not crazy about the mount system. So, it's pretty good, but could be better for this price.
    -Curve (5*) - 1500R seems perfect to me. Not too aggressive, very comfortable.
    -Colors, contrast (5*) - This is the best screen I've used to be honest. I'm not as picky as some people are, so I'm sure some people will have their complaints, but the screen is amazing in comparison to my old monitor (cheap VA panel). I love the QLED, bright colors, great contrast. I'm happy with this part.
    -HDR (1*) - No HDR. This was my fault that I wasn't paying attention though. Still a point against this monitor though.
    -TB3 Docking (1*) - I'm not happy with the TB3 docking. This is one of the primary reasons I bought this monitor over others, but it doesn't completely work. I use this monitor on 3 different computers for different things, so I wanted the docking capability (rather than just buying an expensive separate dock) to make my life easier when switching around all the time. Issue 1, no Freesync through TB3. This isn't exactly Samsung's fault, but that needs to be made clear. Issue 2, the TB cable that it comes with does not support daisy-chaining monitors. If you're going to sell a monitor and advertise that it can daisy-chain, then it had better support daisy-chaining out of the box. This was a ripoff to me. I had to buy a separate cable, which aren't exactly cheap. Issue 3, USB plugs in the monitor don't work properly. I have a couple of devices that work, but most don't, so even though it is in USB hub mode and is supposed to be just the TB3 cable I switch between computers (and leave all peripherals connected to the monitor), this doesn't work and I have a separate USB hub that I move around also. I contacted support about this and the only way to do anything about it would be to ship the monitor to a service center where they would keep it for several days/weeks to service it, but I use this monitor 50-60 hours a week. I can't afford to be without it that long.

    tl;dr - great quality monitor if you're looking for curve, vibrant colors, lots of space, but don't get it for the docking because it doesn't work as well as it should.
