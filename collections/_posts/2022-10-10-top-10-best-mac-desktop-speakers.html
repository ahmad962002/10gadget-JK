    CURRENT PERSPECTIVE:
    Disregarding the fact that these had blown out on me, I currently believe these speakers are not worth the absurdly high price. Although I still believe the mid-range is warm and quite rich, both the low-end and high-end struggle to reproduce sounds nicely. This leaves you with a speaker that lacks clarity on two very crucial frequency ranges. Even after the addition of a subwoofer, the lack of EQ options on the speaker do not allow you to setup an appropriate crossfade, which in turn create bass frequencies that cancel. These speakers still look phenomenal though, and packaging is premium enough to justify a higher price tag, but not $260. For reference, as soon as I refunded my A2+, I went ahead and purchased a pair of KRK RP5 G4 Studio Monitors for the exact same price (These monitors are also fairly controversial, but I personally really enjoy their soundstage and less colored sound).

FAQS

What speakers should I buy for my desktop?

There are many different types of speakers that will work with a desktop computer. The most common type is a set of 2-3 satellite speakers that plug into the computer's USB or 3.5mm audio jack. Other popular types of speakers include Bluetooth wireless speakers and 5.1 surround sound systems. Ultimately, it depends on your budget and what kind of audio experience you're looking for.

Can I use external speakers with an iMac?

Yes, you can use external speakers with an iMac. Just make sure that the appropriate ports are open and that the sound is turned up.

Does iMac Desktop have speakers?

Yes, the iMac desktop includes built-in stereo speakers.

How do I get the best sound out of my Mac?

There is no one perfect answer to this question as every person's ears and Mac setup are different. However, some tips to getting the best sound out of your Mac include making sure that your Mac's audio settings are optimized for your particular audio setup, using headphones or external speakers instead of the built-in speakers where possible, and checking for any software updates that may improve sound quality.
