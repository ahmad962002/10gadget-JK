    The problem with the common man, as I chronicled in my extensive memoirs, is that there are so many of them.

    When the fine timepiece finally stops, the common man A) takes it to the jewelry counter/store to get a new battery, or B) tosses it in the kitchen drawer. You know, THAT drawer, which will prove so fascinating when archeologists dig it up a thousand years from now.
